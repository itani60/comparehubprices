Supabase Meeting Prep

Migration Strategy & Credit Funding Interface
Question 1: The Intro

"Tell us about your product"

Strategy: Pitch the business value clearly. Show it's a real platform, not just a toy.
"I built CompareHubPrices, a price comparison platform for South Africa.

Users can search for products and compare prices from different stores nationwide to find the best deals. The platform includes Price Alerts where users set alerts on products and get notified through browser notifications or email when prices drop. We also have a Wishlist feature where users can enable a setting to automatically add all wishlist products to price alerts, so they never miss a deal on items they're tracking.

We're also launching LocalHub - a dedicated platform within CompareHubPrices that allows legitimate small businesses to advertise and be seen nationwide. Business users can engage with their followers, collect ratings and reviews, and access analytics to understand their base and product performance. LocalHub empowers service-based small businesses - like caterers, salons, restaurants, gift hamper creators, wedding dress designers, and card makers - to grow their reach and connect with customers nationwide.

The platform also includes a comprehensive admin management system for overseeing platform operations:
  • Product Catalogs: Bulk imports and price updates
  • User Accounts & Business Accounts: Managing users, handling reports and support tickets
  • Business Verification: Verifying and approving business posts before they go live on LocalHub, handling reports against businesses (fraud, harassment, illegitimate activities)
  • Analytics: Tracking user activity, logins, and business performance
  • Content Moderation: Reviewing posts, articles, and reviews
  • Platform Monitoring: System health checks and audit logs
"
Question 2: The Setup

"What is your current tech stack?"

Strategy: Set the stage for the migration. Mention specific AWS services.
"Right now, I'm fully on AWS Serverless.
I'm using DynamoDB for data, Cognito for auth, and heavily relying on Lambda functions for even simple logic.

However, the unpredictable costs are a nightmare. I set minimum budgets for monthly testing, but DynamoDB charges me heavily once my free tier usage limit is finished.

I had to backup my data and delete 50 DynamoDB tables to stop the bleeding. I kept only the Lambdas, thinking that would solve it. But I was still getting charged even though I wasn't actively using the services. I also made costly mistakes early on - using scan instead of query which racked up charges quickly until I learned to optimize.

As an unemployed developer, these unexpected bills force me to dip into savings or ask family for help to pay AWS bills. I need a more predictable, affordable solution."
Question 3: The Pitch (Crucial)

"Why do you want to switch to Supabase?"

Strategy: Contrast AWS pain with Supabase benefits. They love hearing this.
"I spend too much time managing IAM roles and debugging NoSQL schemas. I want Supabase because:
  • Relational Data: I need Postgres. Managing complex relationships in DynamoDB is slowing me down.
  • Client-Side Fetching: I want to query data directly from my frontend using your JS client, instead of writing hundreds of middleware Lambdas.
  • Auth: I need an authentication system that just works out of the box, unlike Cognito.

But honestly, the real reason is funding. Even after optimizing everything, I don't see myself running my website on AWS because of lack of funds. I actually like the AWS interface and have had many thoughts about staying, but I'm now in the final stages of deployment and starting to realize that using AWS might make me fail due to lack of funding.

I need Supabase to actually launch this platform and give it a fighting chance.

I'll be honest with you - $300 in credits would make a huge difference to me. I still have to register my business too. Covering me for 12 months gives me the chance to look for funding or save a $300 budget allocated for Supabase. It also reduces my stress tremendously.

I want to turn this into reality, and you guys are my only hope. I might be an amateur with less experience coming to you, but I'm coming with honesty and asking for help to turn my dream into reality."
Question 4: The Scale

"How much traffic/data do you expect?"

Strategy: Be ambitious but realistic about data volume.
"We're storing product catalogs from multiple retailers nationwide - thousands of products with price history tracking. We also handle user accounts, price alerts, wishlists, and search queries.

For LocalHub, businesses upload their service offerings and engage with customers. We need robust database storage for all this data and bandwidth for real-time price updates and notifications.

We are planning to scale aggressively in the South African market over the next 6 months."
Question 5: Usage

"What features will you use?"

  • Database: Postgres for core user and product data.
  • Auth: Replacing Cognito entirely.
  • Storage: Hosting business logos and product images.
  • Edge Functions: For complex tasks like processing those bulk CSV uploads.
  • Realtime: For the chat messaging system between users and businesses.
Feature Deep Dive

"Key Feature: The Chat System"

Strategy: If they ask for a specific use-case, this is your gold mine. It shows you understand the unique value of Supabase.
"One of our core retention features is Chat between Shoppers and Store Owners.

On AWS, building this required API Gateway Websockets + Lambda Triggers + DynamoDB Streams. It was over-engineered.

With Supabase, I can simply subscribe to the `messages` table using Realtime. Only the receiver gets the message (thanks to RLS). This architectural simplification alone is worth the migration."
Tip #1
Be Honest about AWS Pain

They love hearing that AWS is hard to use. It validates their product's existence. Don't be afraid to say "AWS is a nightmare to configure."

Tip #2
Ask a Smart Question

"Do you have any specific guides or tools for migrating data from DynamoDB to Postgres?"
This shows you are ready to move NOW.

Tip #3: Technical Proof (The Closer)
Show you've done your homework

If they ask about Lambda, say this exactly:
"I've already successfully tested connecting my AWS Lambdas to Supabase using the Supavisor Transaction Pooler (port 6543). It works perfectly with my IPv4 environment, so I'm confident I can migrate my backend logic incrementally."
